Welcome to Preston City of Sanctuary!

We are part of a national movement dedicated to building a culture of welcome for people seeking sanctuary in the UK. Our aim is to create a network of people and organisations right across the city, united in making our city more welcoming, open and fair for all, by creating platforms for collaboration, dialogue and mutual opportunities.

About Us

Preston City of Sanctuary is part of a national movement dedicated to building a culture of welcome for people seeking sanctuary in the UK. Our aim is to bring together people and organisations across the city to create a more welcoming, open, and fair Preston for everyone.

We have built a strong network of volunteers, professionals, statutory, voluntary, and faith organisations, working alongside asylum seekers and refugees who live in our city. Through collaboration, dialogue, and shared opportunities, we support new citizens and our multicultural communities through a two-way integration process.

We regularly hold open network meetings, events, and celebrations to strengthen connections and promote inclusion across Preston.

Note: For a general introduction to some of the issues and stories, please watch this now somewhat dated video above.

What We Do

Here in Preston we have built a network of volunteers, professionals, statutory, voluntary and faith organisations, together with asylum seekers and refugees living in our city. We proactively share information about issues, campaigns, organisations and events that support or are of interest to sanctuary seekers.

We are committed to supporting the new citizens and our multicultural communities with the two-way integration process. We hold open meetings of the whole network and other events and celebrations from time to time.

Preston College

The main centre for learning English and getting qualifications is Preston College. Asylum seekers and refugees can register for free courses.

Website: https://www.preston.ac.uk/adults/esol/

Phone: 01772 225522 to organise an assessment date

Fee Information:
  • If you do not work and receive a benefit - courses may be free (bring recent evidence)
  • If you work and individual income is less than £16009.50 - 100% reduction in fees
  • If household income is less than £30,994 - eligible for discount
  • Otherwise fees are £175 for all qualifications

Employment

Refugees can seek employment after registering for NI number.

Job Centre Plus Preston - register for work/benefits

Lancashire Refugee Integration Team (LRIT)

The team coordinates arrivals and support for thousands of people who have entered Lancashire through over 10 different refugee and asylum resettlement routes.

The team has grown to 29 staff (2022) with over 50% having lived experience of being a refugee or asylum seeker.

Current Campaigning

We support the campaigns led by our partner organisation Asylum Matters.

At the moment (September 2025) increasingly restrictive and cruel policies on immigration and asylum are being proposed by opposition parties and far right groups.

Suspension of Refugee Family Reunion

A Statement of Changes to the Immigration Rules has confirmed the suspension of new refugee family reunion applications after 3pm on Thursday 4th September.

This decision puts families at risk of prolonged separation.

Resources:

  • Update from Free Movement
  • GMIAU Explainer
  • RAMFEL Human Impact Analysis
Refugee Citizenship Ban

As of February 2025, anyone who arrived in the UK 'illegally' and/or via 'dangerous routes' will normally be refused British citizenship.

This will impact an estimated 70,000 people.

The Government's ban on refugees obtaining citizenship will leave thousands of torture survivors feeling like second-class citizens.

More information on Asylum Matters website.
